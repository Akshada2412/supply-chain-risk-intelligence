## Executive Summary

Analysis of trade flow data across 50 countries and 5 
product categories (2018–2022) reveals that **Electronics 
represents the highest supply chain risk** to global 
operations, driven by dangerous concentration of Chinese 
suppliers combined with elevated geopolitical exposure.

The COVID-19 shock of 2020 — which erased **$328 billion** 
in global trade in a single year — demonstrated the real 
cost of undiversified supply chains. This memo outlines 
findings and recommended actions.

---

## Key Findings

### Finding 1 — Electronics: Highest Risk Category
- **HHI Score: 650.5 | Combined Risk Score: 3.19/10**
- China supplies 17.2% of global electronics exports
- China carries a geopolitical risk score of 7/10
- A disruption to Chinese electronics supply would impact 
  every major importing economy simultaneously

### Finding 2 — Raw Materials: Hidden Concentration Risk
- **HHI Score: 1,249.6** — highest concentration of all 
  categories
- United States supplies 29.3% of global raw material 
  exports
- While US geo risk is low (2/10), this level of single-
  country dependency is structurally fragile
- Any policy shift, natural disaster, or trade dispute 
  creates immediate downstream impact

### Finding 3 — COVID-19 Exposed Systemic Fragility
- Global imports dropped from **$1.91T (2019) → $1.58T 
  (2020)**
- That is a **$328 billion collapse in 12 months**
- Recovery to pre-COVID levels took until 2021
- Supply chains with high concentration had the slowest 
  recovery times

### Finding 4 — 11 High Risk Supplier Nations Active
- Countries with geo risk ≥ 7 include: China, Russia, 
  Ukraine, Iran, Venezuela, Afghanistan, Syria, Yemen, 
  Iraq, Nigeria, Pakistan
- Combined these nations represent significant import 
  dependency for electronics and raw materials

## Recommendations

### Immediate (0–30 days)
1. **Audit Electronics suppliers** — map all tier-1 and 
   tier-2 suppliers with China exposure
2. **Flag Raw Materials concentration** — initiate 
   secondary supplier qualification in Australia and Canada
3. **Exit or hedge** positions in Russia/Ukraine exposed 
   supply routes

### Short Term (30–90 days)
1. **Diversification roadmap** — shift 15% of Electronics 
   sourcing from China to Vietnam and Malaysia by Q3
2. **Strategic inventory** — build 60-day buffer stock for 
   Semiconductor and Electronics categories
3. **Supplier risk scoring** — implement quarterly HHI 
   monitoring for all product categories

### Long Term (90+ days)
1. **Regional supply hubs** — establish procurement 
   relationships in 3+ regions per product category
2. **Real-time risk dashboard** — deploy this platform 
   organisation-wide for continuous monitoring
3. **Annual supply chain stress test** — simulate 
   disruption scenarios using this HHI framework

## Conclusion

The data is clear — supply chain concentration is not 
a theoretical risk, it is an active vulnerability. 
The $328B COVID trade collapse proved that diversification 
is not optional. The HHI framework deployed in this 
analysis provides a repeatable, data-driven method to 
monitor and manage this risk going forward.

**Recommended next step:** Present findings to COO and 
initiate supplier diversification working group within 
30 days.
